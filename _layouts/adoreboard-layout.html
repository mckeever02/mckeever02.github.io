{% include partials/case-study-h2.html color="altGray-1000" title="Redesigning the adorescore" %}
{% include partials/case-study-p.html color="altGray-900" content="The Adorescore (a metric scored from -100 to 100 which allows you to understand how well or badly your brand is performing) is a pivotal aspect of the Emotics product. Discussions within the team however and feedback from users concluded that too much emphasis was being placed on it and that the score, by itself, wasn't a key indicator of the data." %} {% include partials/case-study-p.html color="altGray-900" content="This was leading to confusion from users who felt that the score was too inaccurate and not reflective of the data." %}
{% include partials/case-study-p.html color="altGray-900" content='To combat this, I tackled two things:' %}
  1. Revisit the hierarchical layout to place less emphasis on the dataset's adorescores from a visual perspective.
  2. Give a visual breakdown of each adorescore that will allow users, at a glance, to distingish if their datasets consists of mostly negative, neutral or positive data.
